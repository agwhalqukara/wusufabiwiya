NBA YoungBoy Career

YoungBoy Never Broke Again, also known as NBA YoungBoy or simply YoungBoy, is an American rapper whose career has been marked by impressive achievements and consistent chart-topping success. His journey began between 2015 and 2017 when he independently released eight mixtapes, steadily building a devoted fan base. In August 2017, he signed with Atlantic Records, a significant turning point in his career.

His single “Outside Today,” dropped in January 2018, reached the 31st spot on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, and became the lead track for his debut studio album, “Until Death Call My Name,” which peaked at number 7 on the US Billboard 200 in 2018.

YoungBoys upward trajectory continued with the release of “Bandit” in October 2019, a collaboration with Juice World that marked his first top-ten single. A week later, he released “AI YoungBoy 2,” which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 in 2019.

In April 2020, “38 Baby 2” became his second chart-topping project on the Billboard 200. Later that year, his second studio album, “Top,” followed suit as his third chart-topping project in less than a year.

An impressive milestone in his career came in September 2021 when “Sincerely, Kentrell” was released during his incarceration, topping the charts. This achievement made him only the third artist, alongside 2Pac and Lil Wayne, to have a chart-topping album while incarcerated.

In August 2022, YoungBoy released his fourth album, “The Last Slimeto,” marking his final album with Atlantic Records. He subsequently signed with Motown Records, ushering in a new phase in his career. 

In January 2023, he dropped his fifth album, “I Rest My Case,” and continued to make waves in the industry by releasing his sixth full-length album, “Don’t Try This at Home,” in April 2023. YoungBoy’s career trajectory showcases his remarkable talent and enduring impact in hip-hop.
