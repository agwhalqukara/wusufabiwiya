GA Rome Fil a Chick Shooting: Anthony Green

The Chick-Fil-A restaurant in Rome, Georgia, was in the news for the death of two people in its parking area. Cassie Davis, a resident of Rome, was found dead in her car, while her ex-boyfriend, Anthony Green, aged 56, also succumbed to bullet injuries. The police have notified it as a case of shooting resulting in a murder-suicide.

The preliminary investigation found that Cassie’s ex-boyfriend was waiting in the fast food restaurant’s drive-thru and later fired at her. According to the police report, three bullets were pumped into her while she was in the driving seat. The assailant then turned the gun towards himself and fired the final bullet.

Family Reaction

The family of Cassie has reacted to the shooting incident and blamed the jealous ex-boyfriend for her death. Cassie’s mother, Kathie Walters, said she never thought her daughter would be shot and killed by her ex-boyfriend. The family described Cassie, a mother of seven kids, as a sweet lady with many friends.

Restaurant Statement

The Chick-Fil-A restaurant in Rome, Georgia, issued a statement regarding the incident. It said, “A horrific incident has occurred outside our restaurant, and we are cooperating with police in the investigation process.”

It added that no staff members were hurt in the incident, but they mourn with the community and will remain closed for one day for repair work.
